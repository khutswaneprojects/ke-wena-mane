The Change Cycle

Set goal → Perceive reality through that lens → Notice relevant information → Act and get feedback → Repeat until automatic → Become "who you are" → Defend this identity → Identity shapes new goals.

This cycle often starts in childhood through conditioning. You survive ideologically after physical survival needs are met.

Stages of Mind Development

  • Most readers are between stages 4-8 (Self-Aware to Construct-Aware).
  • Progress requires moving through predictable stages.
  • Higher stages involve questioning inherited beliefs and building personal principles.

Intelligence = ability to get what you want from life.

Requires: Agency + Opportunity + Intelligence (cybernetics).

Part 1: Morning - Psychological Excavation

15-30 minutes for self-reflection

Anti-Vision Questions:

  1. What dissatisfaction have you learned to tolerate?
  2. What do you complain about but never change?
  3. What truth about your life would be unbearable to admit?
  4. Describe your life in 5 years if nothing changes (typical Tuesday).
  5. Describe it at 10 years - what opportunities closed?
  6. End of life perspective - what did you never try?
  7. What identity must you give up to change?
  8. What's the embarrassing real reason you haven't changed?

Vision Questions:

  1. What beliefs about yourself would make that life feel natural?
  2. What would you do this week if you were already that person?

The One-Day Protocol

Part 2: Throughout Day - Pattern Interruption

Integration Questions:

  • Why have you been stuck?
  • What is the actual internal enemy (pattern/belief)?
  • One sentence capturing what you refuse to let your life become.
  • One sentence capturing what you're building toward.

Goal Setting (as lenses, not finish lines):

  • 1-year goal: What must be true to know you've broken the pattern?
  • 1 month: What must be true to keep you break the pattern?
  • Daily: What 2-3 actions would you take to ensure you're still breaking the pattern?
